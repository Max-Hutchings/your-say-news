Suggested default

Option 1

Vertical list

One row per characteristic, all visible at once. Each row previews its group count and a tiny lean-bar, so you know what you'll get before tapping.

Option 2

Two-column grid

Compact cards, two across. Roughly twice the density of the scroll, all visible without swiping, and it stays tidy as axes grow.

Best for: a balance of density and calm. Keeps the tap targets big. Watch: less room for a per-item preview than the list.

Option 3

Grouped sections

Chips organised under headings. The one that scales cleanly to the 36 characteristics the backend already supports — you'd add rows, not clutter.

Option 4

Wrapping chips

The same pills you have now, but wrapped to fill the width instead of scrolling. The smallest change — every option visible, nothing else moves.

Best for: the lowest-effort win — reuses the existing chip, just removes the scroll. Watch: no room for previews or grouping; a 36-axis future gets busy.

Recommendation

Start with Option 1 (Vertical list) — it solves the real problem (everything visible + a preview of what each split looks like) and feels considered. If vertical height in the sheet is tight, Option 4 (Wrapping chips) is the near-zero-effort fallback, and Option 3 (Sections) is where to head once you surface more of the 36 backend characteristics.
